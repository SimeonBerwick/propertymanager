# DECISION_FRAMEWORK.md

## Purpose
Use this framework for meaningful projects, purchases, hires, partnerships, product ideas, and operational changes.

## Step 1: Define the decision
Write down:
- The decision to make
- Why it matters now
- What success looks like
- Deadline, if any

## Step 2: Evaluate from each executive lens

### Mario Lens — Strategy
Ask:
- Does this move us toward the long-term vision?
- Is this leverage or noise?
- Does this improve positioning?
- What happens if we do nothing?

### Elon Lens — First Principles / Technology
Ask:
- What assumptions are we blindly inheriting?
- What is the simplest true system?
- Can this be automated, simplified, or rebuilt better?
- What technical risks are real versus imagined?

### Warren Lens — Finance / Stability
Ask:
- What does this cost now?
- What does it cost to maintain?
- What does it do to cash flow and downside risk?
- Is this durable or fragile?

### Barry Bot Lens — Execution / Coordination
Ask:
- What are the next concrete steps?
- Who owns each step?
- What can block this?
- What should be documented, tracked, or delegated?

## Step 3: Score the decision
Simple 1–5 scale:
- Strategic fit
- Technical feasibility
- Financial soundness
- Operational clarity

## Step 4: Choose a mode
- **Execute now** — clear upside, manageable downside
- **Stage-gate** — proceed only after proof or milestones
- **Defer** — good idea, wrong time
- **Reject** — weak strategy, poor economics, or bad distraction

## Step 5: Translate into action
Every approved decision should create:
- owner
- deadline
- success metric
- Kanban task(s)
- review checkpoint
